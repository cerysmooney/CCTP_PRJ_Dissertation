How do Noise and Colour Connect?

Coloured noise is a way for us to understand the frequency patterns of both noise and light together.

Take white for example. Just as the colour white contains all colours of the rainbow, white noise contains every audible frequency.

We can use a Power Spectral Density (PSD) chart to visualise more coloured noises!

Power Spectral Density

A PSD chart allows us to see the distribution of frequencies (vibrations) in noise. X shows us the specific frequency Y shows us the presence at the specfic frequency

Noises with higher present frequencies are associated with higher light frequencies, such as violet (with a light spectrum of 400nm).

Similarly, noises with lower present frequencies are connected with lower light frequencies, such as red (with a light spectrum of 700nm).
