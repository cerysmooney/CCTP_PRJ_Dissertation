Brown Noise

Much like pink noise, brown noise has significantly more audible tones when in lower frequencies.

Brown noise gets its name from Robert Brown, a scientist who noticed something interesting while looking at tiny particles floating in water. These particles seemed to move around randomly, without any obvious reason. This random movement became known as Brownian motion.

Brown Noise on the PSD Chart

We can see on the PSD chart that it decreases in energy as the frequencies get higher.

This means that brown noise contains more audible sounds in lower pitches.

What does it sound like?

With its strong presence in lower frequencies, brown noise can sound like a thunderstorm or a roaring waterfall!
