White Noise

White Noise is created by combining all of the audible sound frequencies at once.

This means that every frequency (ranging from 20 Hertz to 20,000 Hertz) can be heard at an equal level!

What does it sound like?

White noise can be compared to listening to TV static or a vaccuum buzzing.

Hover over the chameleon to hear for yourself!

How did White Noise get its name?

If we look at the colour white, we can see that it contains all visible colours to the human eye.

From listening to this noise, we hear that it contains all audible frequencies that humans can hear.

It is called White Noise simply by the connection of the two functionalities!
