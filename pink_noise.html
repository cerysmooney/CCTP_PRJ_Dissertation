Pink Noise

In Pink Noise, lower frequencies are much more audible than higher ones.

With these deeper tones emphazised, we can imagine that this noise (if visible) would have a warmer colour to it, giving it the name Pink Noise!

Pink Noise on a PSD Chart

A PSD chart allows us to see the distribution of frequencies (vibrations) in noise.

X shows us the specific frequency and Y shows us the presence at the specfic frequency

Noises with higher present frequencies are associated with higher light frequencies, such as violet (with a light spectrum of 400nm).

Similarly, noises with lower present frequencies are connected with lower light frequencies, such as red (with a light spectrum of 700nm).
